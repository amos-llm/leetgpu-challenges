Implement Adaptive Layer Normalization (AdaLN) — the conditioning mechanism used in modern Diffusion Transformers (DiT, Stable Diffusion 3, FLUX, OpenSora, SiT). Each token is layer-normalized along its feature dimension, then modulated by a per-batch scale and shift vector that comes from a separate conditioning network (e.g. timestep / class embedding).

Given an input X of shape [B, N, D], a per-batch scale scale of shape [B, D], and a per-batch shift shift of shape [B, D], compute for every (b, n, d): \[ \begin{aligned} \mu_{b,n} &= \frac{1}{D}\sum_{d=0}^{D-1} X_{b,n,d} \\ \sigma^{2}_{b,n} &= \frac{1}{D}\sum_{d=0}^{D-1} (X_{b,n,d} - \mu_{b,n})^{2} \\ \hat{X}_{b,n,d} &= \frac{X_{b,n,d} - \mu_{b,n}}{\sqrt{\sigma^{2}_{b,n} + \epsilon}} \\ Y_{b,n,d} &= \hat{X}_{b,n,d} \cdot (1 + \text{scale}_{b,d}) + \text{shift}_{b,d} \end{aligned} \] Variance is computed with the biased (population) estimator, and \(\epsilon\) is fixed at 1e-5. Note that the same scale and shift vectors are broadcast across all N tokens within a batch element.

Implementation Requirements

  • Implement the solve function with the signature unchanged.
  • Do not use external libraries beyond the framework provided.
  • Write the result into output in-place.

Example

Input: B = 1, N = 2, D = 4

\(X\) (float32, \(1 \times 2 \times 4\)): \[ X = \begin{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 2.0 & 3.0 & 4.0 \\ 4.0 & 3.0 & 2.0 & 1.0 \end{bmatrix} \end{bmatrix} \] \(\text{scale}\) (\(1 \times 4\)): \[ \text{scale} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.0 & 1.0 & 0.0 & -0.5 \end{bmatrix} \] \(\text{shift}\) (\(1 \times 4\)): \[ \text{shift} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.0 & 0.0 & 1.0 & 0.0 \end{bmatrix} \]

Both rows have mean \(\mu = 2.5\) and variance \(\sigma^{2} = 1.25\), so \(1/\sqrt{\sigma^{2} + \epsilon} \approx 0.8944\). After normalization: \[ \hat{X} \approx \begin{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1.3416 & -0.4472 & 0.4472 & 1.3416 \\ 1.3416 & 0.4472 & -0.4472 & -1.3416 \end{bmatrix} \end{bmatrix} \] Applying \((1 + \text{scale}) = (1.0,\, 2.0,\, 1.0,\, 0.5)\) and adding \(\text{shift} = (0.0,\, 0.0,\, 1.0,\, 0.0)\) gives: \[ \text{output} \approx \begin{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1.3416 & -0.8944 & 1.4472 & 0.6708 \\ 1.3416 & 0.8944 & 0.5528 & -0.6708 \end{bmatrix} \end{bmatrix} \]

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ B ≤ 64
  • 1 ≤ N ≤ 8,192
  • 2 ≤ D ≤ 4,096
  • All tensors are float32 on the GPU.
  • Input values are in the range [-10.0, 10.0].
  • scale and shift values are in the range [-1.0, 1.0].
  • eps is fixed at 1e-5.
  • Performance is measured with B = 16, N = 4,096, D = 1,152
