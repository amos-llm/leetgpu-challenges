Implement a fused GPU kernel that applies Rotary Positional Embedding (RoPE) to query and key tensors, then immediately uses the rotated results to compute scaled dot-product attention. Inputs are float32 tensors: Q, K, and V all have shape (M, D) (self-attention), while cos and sin are the RoPE frequency components shared by Q and K. Fusing RoPE into the attention kernel saves two full read–write passes over the (M, D) intermediate tensors, reducing global memory bandwidth by roughly a third.

For each pair of tokens i, j, the kernel computes:

\[ \text{RoPE}(x, c, s)_i = x_i \odot c_i + \text{rotate\_half}(x_i) \odot s_i \] \[ \text{rotate\_half}([x_1, \dots, x_{d/2}, x_{d/2+1}, \dots, x_d]) = [-x_{d/2+1}, \dots, -x_d, x_1, \dots, x_{d/2}] \] \[ \text{attn}_{i,j} = \frac{\exp(\text{RoPE}(Q, \text{cos}, \text{sin})_i \cdot \text{RoPE}(K, \text{cos}, \text{sin})_j^T \,/\, \sqrt{D})} {\sum_{k=0}^{M-1} \exp(\text{RoPE}(Q, \text{cos}, \text{sin})_i \cdot \text{RoPE}(K, \text{cos}, \text{sin})_k^T \,/\, \sqrt{D})} \] \[ \text{out}_i = \sum_{j=0}^{M-1} \text{attn}_{i,j} \cdot V_j \]

The dotted rope_q and rope_k boxes above represent intermediate values that should live only in registers or shared memory — never written to global memory.

Implementation Requirements

  • Do not change the function signature.
  • Do not use external libraries beyond what is available in the starter.
  • D is guaranteed to be an even number.
  • All computation must occur on the GPU.
  • The kernel must fuse the RoPE rotation of Q and K with the attention computation, without writing intermediate rotated tensors to global memory.

Example

M = 2, D = 2. With identity rotation (cos = 1, sin = 0) the output matches standard attention:

\[ Q = \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 0.0 \\ 0.0 & 1.0 \end{bmatrix} \quad K = \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 0.0 \\ 0.0 & 1.0 \end{bmatrix} \quad V = \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 2.0 \\ 3.0 & 4.0 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ \text{cos} = \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 1.0 \\ 1.0 & 1.0 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{sin} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.0 & 0.0 \\ 0.0 & 0.0 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ \text{out} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 1.6604 & 2.6604 \\ 2.3396 & 3.3396 \end{bmatrix} \]

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ M ≤ 65,536
  • 2 ≤ D ≤ 1,024 (even number)
  • Q, K, V, cos, sin, and output are float32
  • Performance is measured with M = 1,024, D = 128
